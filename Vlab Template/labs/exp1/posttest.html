BREADBOARD

Post Test

Q1.What is the gap that separates the two sides of the breadboard called?
  (A) The dip
  (B) The rail
  (C) The switch
  (D) The ravine
Ans is (D)


Q2.Similarity between PCB and Bread board is
  (A) Both allow to design temporary circuits
  (B) Both can be used for solderless components
  (C) Complex circuits can be designed on both
  (D) Both can handle high current
Ans is (C)


Q3.Both can handle high current
  (A) Contain buss strips commonly used for power connections
  (B) Provide socket strips in the build area to hold components
  (C) Are conductive with low resistance
  (D) All of the above
Ans is (D)


Q4.When connecting bus or socket strips the jumper wires should be
  (A) Long enough to make the connection yet short enough to prevent complications
  (B) Wrapped in a coil to prevent interference with the other components
  (C) As long at the test instrument’s leads to decrease resistance
  (D) Soldered to the components to ensure conductivity
Ans is (A)


Q5.Breadboards allow for prototyping an electrical circuit without the need to solder the components
  (A) True
  (B) False
Ans is (A)


Q6.Components installed in parallel on a breadboard will share the same socket strip at both ends
  (A) True
  (B) False
Ans is (C)


Q7.Using a breadboard helps in
  (A) Soldering different components
  (B) Checking function of circuit
  (C) Checking design of circuit
  (D) Both b and c
Ans is (D)


Q8.The maximum frequency that can be handled by breadboard is
  (A) 10 MHz
  (B) 10 GHz
  (C) 10 Hz
  (D) 10 KHz
Ans is (A)
Q9.Using a breadboard helps in
  (A) Soldering different components
  (B) Checking function of circuit
  (C) Checking design of circuit
  (D) Both b and c
Ans is (D)

Q10.In preparing to build on a breadboard one should
  (A) Simply look at the schematic and begin the build
  (B) Never assume the test equipment and components are functional
  (C) Ignore any burn marks as you are only experimenting on the circuit
  (D) Assume all jumper wires and connectors are intact
Ans is (B)
